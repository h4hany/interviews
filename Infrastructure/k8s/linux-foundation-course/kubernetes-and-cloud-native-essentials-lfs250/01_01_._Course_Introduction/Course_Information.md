# Course Introduction

If you’re new to modern application development and deployment, this course is the perfect place to start. You’ll get a clear, practical introduction to cloud native technologies and see how Kubernetes, the leading open source container orchestration platform, helps bring them to life.

Kubernetes has become a core skill in today’s IT landscape, powering everything from small applications to large-scale production systems. Yet with so many tools and frameworks available, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed. This course helps you cut through the noise by showing how Kubernetes provides a flexible framework for scaling, load balancing, orchestration, self-healing, and configuration management—once you know how to use it effectively.

Whether you’re managing clusters, deploying applications, or helping your organization shift toward modern cloud architectures, you’ll gain the foundational knowledge to understand how Kubernetes works, what problems it solves, and how it fits into the broader ecosystem. By the end, you’ll be confident navigating Kubernetes environments and familiar with key extensions such as monitoring and application delivery tools that complete the cloud native picture.

By the end of this course, you will learn the following:

  * Discuss the basics of cloud native technologies.
  * Understand the high-level architecture of Kubernetes.
  * Deal with the challenges of container orchestration.
  * Discuss how container orchestration differs from legacy deployments.
  * Deliver and monitor your application in a distributed system.



You should expect to spend approximately 8-10 hours to complete this course. You have access to this course for 12 months from the date you registered, or until your subscription expires (whichever happens first).

This course is self-paced, allowing you to learn at your own pace. You can pause anytime and resume exactly where you left off—no deadlines, no pressure. To get the most out of your learning experience, we recommend studying regularly rather than taking long breaks, as this helps reinforce understanding and improve retention.

Each chapter concludes with a set of knowledge check questions designed to help you reinforce your understanding of key concepts and assess your comprehension of the material. These questions are not graded but serve as a valuable learning tool. Upon completing all chapters, you will be required to take a final exam, and a score of 70% or higher will be necessary to successfully complete the course.
